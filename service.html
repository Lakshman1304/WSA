SERVICES

RESCUE OF INJURED

Rescuing injured dogs can be a challenging and emotional task, but it's important to approach the situation calmly and with care. The first step is to assess the dog's condition and determine if it's safe to approach. If the dog is aggressive or frightened, it may be necessary to call animal control or a trained professional. If the dog is approachable, use caution and avoid making sudden movements.

OLD STRAY ANIMALS

Caring for old or stray dogs can be a rewarding but challenging experience. Older dogs may require more frequent vet visits and specialized diets, while stray dogs may need to be socialized and trained before they can be adopted. It's important to provide a safe and comfortable environment for these dogs, with plenty of food, water, and shelter. If possible, try to establish a routine to help the dogs feel secure.

MEDICAL TREATMENT

Just like humans, dogs may require medical treatment at some point in their lives. This can include routine checkups, vaccinations, and treatment for illnesses or injuries. It's important to establish a relationship with a trusted veterinarian and schedule regular appointments to keep your dog healthy. If your dog shows signs of illness or injury, seek veterinary care promptly to prevent the condition from worsening.
